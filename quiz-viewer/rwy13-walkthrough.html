Why “midfield left downwind to RWY 13” = East

Cooperstown / Figure 26 · UA.V.A.K3 — stay here until this clicks

Step 1 — What “RWY 13” means Runway numbers are the magnetic heading you’d fly on final, rounded to the nearest 10°, drop the zero.
RWY 13 → about 130° → roughly southeast. So landing aircraft fly toward the southeast end of the runway.
Step 2 — Picture the runway as a strip Align the strip NW ↔ SE. Landing on 13 = entering from the NW end, traveling SE.
Step 3 — “Left traffic” is relative to the landing direction Stand facing the way a landing plane flies (toward SE on RWY 13). Your left hand points toward the northeast / east side of the runway. That’s where the whole left pattern lives (downwind, base, etc.).
Step 4 — What “downwind” adds Downwind = parallel to the runway, opposite the landing heading. So on left downwind to 13 you’re on the east side, flying roughly northwest. “Midfield” just means halfway along the runway length — still on that same east side.
Step 5 — Match the answer choices The exam asks where the aircraft is relative to the runway (which side), not which way it’s pointed.
East side → A
South would be past the SE end (final/upwind area), not midfield downwind.
West would be right traffic for RWY 13.
Answer: A — East

Quick check on exam day: RWY number → landing heading → face that way → left side = pattern side. For 13, left ≈ east.

Tiny drill (no chart)

Left downwind to RWY 27 (landing westbound) — which side of the runway?
Face west → left is south → aircraft is South of the runway.
Left downwind to RWY 36 (landing northbound) — which side?
Face north → left is west → aircraft is West of the runway.
