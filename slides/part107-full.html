UA.I.A · General

I.A · 1/2

Task I.A — General

UA.I.A.K1
Applicability of 14 CFR part 107 to small unmanned aircraft operations. Part 107 covers civil small UAS (<55 lb) in the NAS. Hobby/recreational has different rules (49 USC 44809). Public aircraft ops may use other authorities.
UA.I.A.K2
Definitions used in 14 CFR part 107. Know: small UA, sUAS, remote PIC, person manipulating controls, visual observer, control station, uninvolved person, open-air assembly.
UA.I.A.K3
The ramifications of falsification, reproduction, or alteration of a certificate, rating, authorization, record, or report. Falsifying/reproducing/altering certificates, ratings, authorizations, records, or reports can mean denial, suspension, revocation, and civil/criminal penalties.
UA.I.A.K4
Accident reporting. Report to FAA within 10 calendar days if: serious injury / loss of consciousness, OR property damage (other than the UA) with repair cost or fair market value ≥ $500.

UA.I.A · General

I.A · 2/2

Task I.A — General

UA.I.A.K5
Inspection, testing, and demonstration of compliance. You must allow FAA inspection/testing and demonstrate compliance when requested. Keep required docs available: certificate, ID, registration, waiver if any.
UA.I.A.K6
Multiple category sUAS. An aircraft may be eligible in more than one over-people category if it meets each category’s requirements.
UA.I.A.K7
Record retention. Retain required records (e.g., Category 4 maintenance records) for the periods in the regs — typically at least 3 years where specified for Cat 4.
UA.I.A.K8
Previously manufactured sUAS. Previously manufactured aircraft may have paths into over-people categories via Declaration of Compliance / Means of Compliance rules.

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 1/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K1
Registration requirements for sUAS. Register at faadronezone.faa.gov. Mark registration number on the aircraft. Foreign civil aircraft have different marking/registration rules.
UA.I.B.K2
Requirement for the sUAS to be in a condition for safe operation. No flight unless aircraft is in a condition for safe operation. Preflight inspection required every flight.
UA.I.B.K3
Medical condition(s) that would interfere with safe operation of an sUAS. No medical certificate required — but you may not fly (or act as VO) if a physical/mental condition makes safe ops unsafe.
UA.I.B.K4
Responsibility and authority of the remote PIC. Remote PIC is directly responsible for and is the final authority on the operation.

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 2/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K4a
a. Allowing a person other than the remote PIC to manipulate the flight controls Someone else may manipulate controls if the remote PIC can immediately take direct control and remains responsible.
UA.I.B.K5
Regulatory deviation and reporting requirements for in-flight emergencies. In an in-flight emergency, remote PIC may deviate from Part 107 as required. If ATC asks, send a written report.
UA.I.B.K6
Hazardous operations. No careless or reckless operation. No operation that endangers life or property.
UA.I.B.K6a
a. Careless or reckless Careless/reckless = endangering the life or property of another.

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 3/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K6b
b. Dropping an object May drop objects only if it does not create a hazard to persons or property.
UA.I.B.K7
Operating from a moving aircraft or moving land- or water-borne vehicle. From a moving land/water vehicle: only over sparsely populated areas. Never from a moving aircraft. No property carriage for hire from a moving vehicle.
UA.I.B.K8
Alcohol or drugs and the provisions on prohibition of use. No alcohol while on duty or within 8 hours of duty; BAC < 0.04%. No drugs that affect safety. Refusal of testing has certificate consequences.
UA.I.B.K9
Daylight operations. Daylight ops allowed. Civil twilight and night allowed with anti-collision lighting visible ≥ 3 SM (intensity may be reduced for safety).

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 4/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K10
Visual line of sight (VLOS) aircraft operations. VLOS with unaided vision (corrective lenses OK). Brief interruptions OK (e.g., glance at controller) if VLOS regained promptly. FPV alone ≠ VLOS.
UA.I.B.K11
Requirements when a visual observer is used. If using a VO: VO must maintain constant visual contact and communicate effectively with remote PIC. Remote PIC still responsible.
UA.I.B.K12
Prohibition of operating multiple sUAS. One remote PIC → one sUAS at a time (unless waived).
UA.I.B.K13
Prohibition of carrying hazardous material. No carrying hazardous material as defined in 49 CFR.

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 5/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K14
Staying safely away from other aircraft and right-of-way rules. Always yield right-of-way to all aircraft, airborne vehicles, and launch/reentry vehicles. Don’t create a collision hazard.
UA.I.B.K14a
a. See and avoid other aircraft and other potential hazard considerations of the remote PIC See-and-avoid is continuous. Know your UA’s position, attitude, altitude, and direction of flight at all times.
UA.I.B.K15
Operations over human beings (Refer to Area of Operation I, Task E). Ops over people only under Categories 1–4 eligibility (see Task E) or with a waiver.
UA.I.B.K16
Prior authorization required for operation in certain airspace. ATC authorization required before Class B, C, D, or surface Class E designated for an airport. Class G: no ATC auth needed.

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 6/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K17
Operating in the vicinity of airports. Near airports: don’t interfere with airport ops, traffic patterns, or manned traffic. Monitor CTAF/tower when useful for SA.
UA.I.B.K18
Operating in prohibited or restricted areas. No ops in prohibited or restricted areas unless permission from using/controlling agency.
UA.I.B.K19
Flight restrictions in the proximity of certain areas designated by notice to airmen (NOTAM). Comply with TFRs and other NOTAM flight restrictions. Check before flight (e.g., tfr.faa.gov, NOTAMs).
UA.I.B.K20
Preflight familiarization, inspection, and actions for aircraft operations. Assess operating environment, local weather, airspace, persons/property, other aircraft; inspect aircraft; ensure docs available.

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 7/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K21
Operating limitations for sUAS. Hard limits: ≤ 87 kt groundspeed; ≤ 400 ft AGL (or within 400 ft of a structure); ≥ 3 SM visibility from CS; clouds: ≥ 500 ft below and ≥ 2,000 ft horizontal.
UA.I.B.K21a
a. Maximum groundspeed Max groundspeed 87 knots (100 mph).
UA.I.B.K21b
b. Altitude limitations Max 400 ft AGL unless within 400 ft of a structure’s uppermost limit.
UA.I.B.K21c
c. Minimum visibility Minimum visibility 3 statute miles from the control station.

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 8/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K21d
d. Cloud clearance requirements Stay ≥ 500 ft below clouds and ≥ 2,000 ft horizontally. No flying above clouds that block VLOS to the UA.
UA.I.B.K22
Requirements for a Remote Pilot Certificate with an sUAS rating. Remote Pilot Certificate with sUAS rating required to act as remote PIC (with listed exceptions for training under supervision).
UA.I.B.K23
Automated operations. 3 Autonomous flight OK if remote PIC can intervene — still must meet VLOS, yield, and other Part 107 rules.
UA.I.B.K24
Civil twilight operations. Civil twilight: anti-collision lighting ≥ 3 SM required.

UA.I.B · Operating Rules

I.B · 9/9

Task I.B — Operating Rules

UA.I.B.K25
Night operations. Night: anti-collision lighting ≥ 3 SM; knowledge test/recurrent must cover night topics for privileges.
UA.I.B.K26
Transportation of property. May transport property for compensation if within Part 107 limits; not from a moving vehicle; total UA weight still < 55 lb.
UA.I.B.K27
ATC transponder equipment prohibition. sUAS under Part 107 generally may not use ATC transponders unless authorized/required in specific cases — Part 107 prohibits operating with a Mode C/S style ATC transponder unless ATC authorizes.
UA.I.B.K28
ADS-B Out prohibition. ADS-B Out equipment generally prohibited for Part 107 sUAS unless authorized. Don’t confuse with Remote ID broadcast.

I.C

Task I.C — Remote Pilot Certification

UA.I.C.K1
Offenses involving alcohol or drugs. Alcohol/drug offenses can affect certificate eligibility and lead to denial/suspension/revocation.
UA.I.C.K2
The consequences of refusing to submit to a drug or alcohol test or to furnish test results. Refusing a drug/alcohol test or refusing to furnish results can have the same certificate consequences as a failure.

I.E · 1/6

Task I.E — Operations Over People

UA.I.E.K1
Remote pilot responsibilities when operating over people. Remote PIC must ensure the operation meets the category eligibility throughout the flight and follows operating instructions.
UA.I.E.K2
Operations over people at night. Ops over people at night still need category eligibility PLUS night lighting requirements.
UA.I.E.K3
Category of operations, including: Four categories with different weight/injury-risk / airworthiness paths.
UA.I.E.K3a
a. Category 1 Cat 1: ≤ 0.55 lb (including everything aboard); no exposed rotating parts that could lacerate; eligible for sustained flight over open-air assemblies if Remote ID met.

UA.I.E · Operations Over People

I.E · 2/6

Task I.E — Operations Over People

UA.I.E.K3b
b. Category 2 Cat 2: MoC + DoC; injury severity limits; eligible for sustained flight over open-air assemblies (with Remote ID).
UA.I.E.K3c
c. Category 3 Cat 3: MoC + DoC; higher injury thresholds than Cat 2; NOT allowed for sustained flight over open-air assemblies; often closed/restricted sites or limited exposure.
UA.I.E.K3d
d. Category 4 Cat 4: type-certificated aircraft operated per Part 107 with applicable operating limitations; may operate over open-air assemblies if limitations allow.

UA.I.E · Operations Over People

Minimum distances from a person. Minimum distances / exposure limits depend on category and whether people are participating / inside structures / closed sites.
UA.I.E.K6
Operations over moving vehicles. Ops over moving vehicles generally restricted unless everyone is within a closed/restricted-access site or other category conditions met.
UA.I.E.K7
Modifications to an sUAS. Modifications can void category eligibility — follow manufacturer operating instructions and MoC/DoC constraints.
UA.I.E.K8
Closed and restricted access sites. Closed- or restricted-access sites can enable Cat 3 ops where people are notified and able to expect UA overhead.

UA.I.E · Operations Over People

I.E · 4/6

Task I.E — Operations Over People

UA.I.E.K9
Remote Pilot operating instructions. Follow remote pilot operating instructions for that make/model when required for category eligibility.
UA.I.E.K10
Required components and Category declaration. Required components listed in DoC / operating instructions must be installed and functioning.
UA.I.E.K11
Optional components. Optional components only if allowed by operating instructions without changing category performance.
UA.I.E.K12
Applicant produced, designed, or modified sUAS for operations over people. Applicant-designed/modified aircraft need proper MoC/DoC path before Cat 2/3 ops.

Task I.E — Operations Over People

Declaration of Compliance (DoC). Declaration of Compliance (DoC) submitted to FAA for Cat 2/3 eligibility.
UA.I.E.K14
Maintenance of an sUAS that is eligible for operations over people. Maintain aircraft so it continues to meet the category standards; document as required.

Means of Compliance (MoC). Means of Compliance (MoC) = FAA-accepted method showing injury/kinetic energy / safety standards are met.

UA.II.A · Airspace Classification

II.A · 1/3

Task II.A — Airspace Classification

UA.II.A.K1
General airspace: Controlled vs uncontrolled; regulatory vs nonregulatory. For Part 107 focus on B, C, D, E, G.
UA.II.A.K1a
a. Class B controlled airspace Class B: busiest airports; surface to ~10,000 MSL; layered cake; ATC auth required for sUAS.
UA.II.A.K1b
b. Class C controlled airspace Class C: surface ~5 NM + outer shelf ~10 NM to ~4,000 AGL (charted MSL); ATC auth required.
UA.II.A.K1c
c. Class D controlled airspace Class D: towered airports to ~2,500 AGL (charted MSL); dashed blue; ATC auth required.

UA.II.A · Airspace Classification

II.A · 2/3

Task II.A — Airspace Classification

UA.II.A.K1d
d. Class E controlled airspace Class E: controlled leftover. Bases often 700 AGL (magenta vignette) or 1,200 AGL (blue vignette); sometimes surface (dashed magenta). Surface E for airport needs auth.
UA.II.A.K1e
e. Class G uncontrolled airspace Class G: uncontrolled from surface up to overlying E. No ATC auth for Part 107 (still obey altitude/VLOS/weather mins).
UA.II.A.K2
Special-use airspace, such as prohibited, restricted, warning areas, military operation areas, alert areas, and controlled firing areas. P = prohibited (stay out). R = restricted (permission/avoid when hot). W = warning. MOA = military ops (VFR OK, caution when active). A = alert. CFA = not charted; activities stop for nonparticipants.
UA.II.A.K3
Other airspace areas, such as Airport Advisory Services, Military Training Routes (MTRs), Temporary Flight Restrictions (TFRs), Parachute Jump Operations, Terminal Radar Service Areas (TRSAs), National Security Areas (NSA) and Visual Flight Rules (VFR) routes. Also know: MTRs (IR/VR), TFRs, parachute ops, published VFR routes, TRSA, NSA, LAA.

Task II.A — Airspace Classification

UA.II.A.K4
Air Traffic Control (ATC) and the NAS. ATC separates IFR and provides services by airspace class. sUAS enters controlled airspace only with authorization (LAANC/DroneZone/ATC).

Task II.B — Airspace Operational Requirements

UA.III.A · Sources of Weather

III.A · 1/2

Task III.A — Sources of Weather

UA.III.A.K1
Internet weather briefing and sources of weather available for flight planning purposes. aviationweather.gov, 1800wxbrief, AWOS/ASOS phone/radio, apps feeding official METARs/TAFs. Brief before flight.
UA.III.A.K2
Aviation routine weather reports (METAR). METAR = routine hourly observation (SPECI = special). Decode wind, vis (SM), weather, sky (AGL hundreds), temp/dew (°C), altimeter.
UA.III.A.K3
Terminal aerodrome forecasts (TAF). TAF = aerodrome forecast, usually 24–30 hours. FM/TEMPO/PROB/BECMG groups. Use for mission timing.
UA.III.A.K4
Weather charts. Surface analysis, prog charts, weather depiction, radar/satellite — big picture for fronts and systems.

UA.III.A · Sources of Weather

III.A · 2/2

Task III.A — Sources of Weather

UA.III.A.K5
Automated surface observing systems (ASOS) and automated weather observing systems (AWOS). ASOS/AWOS provide automated local observations — great for airport vicinity decisions.

UA.III.B · Effects on Performance

III.B · 1/3

Task III.B — Effects on Performance

UA.III.B.K1
Weather factors and their effects on performance. Weather changes performance, VLOS, and battery endurance. Go/no-go using forecasts + local observations.
UA.III.B.K1a
a. Density altitude High density altitude (hot/high/humid) = thin air = worse climb/endurance/payload capability.
UA.III.B.K1b
b. Wind and currents Wind/gusts/turbulence/microbursts. Gusts above aircraft rating = no-go even if sustained wind OK.
UA.III.B.K1c
c. Atmospheric stability, pressure, and temperature Stable vs unstable: stable = smooth, poor vis; unstable = turbulence, showery precip. Pressure/temp drive density.

UA.III.B · Effects on Performance

III.B · 2/3

Task III.B — Effects on Performance

UA.III.B.K1d
d. Air masses and fronts Fronts bring wind shifts, precip, clouds. Cold fronts often more convective; warm fronts more stratiform/IFR.
UA.III.B.K1e
e. Thunderstorms and microbursts Thunderstorms: avoid. Microbursts = violent downdrafts/outflow near the surface.
UA.III.B.K1f
f. Tornadoes Tornadoes: extreme winds — do not operate.
UA.III.B.K1g
g. Icing Icing degrades lift/prop efficiency; visible moisture + freezing temps = icing risk.

UA.III.B · Effects on Performance

III.B · 3/3

Task III.B — Effects on Performance

UA.III.B.K1h
h. Hail Hail damages airframes/props — associated with thunderstorms.
UA.III.B.K1i
i. Fog Fog when temp/dew point close; radiation fog on clear calm nights; advection fog with moist air over cool surfaces.
UA.III.B.K1j
j. Ceiling and visibility Ceiling = AGL height of lowest broken/overcast. Part 107 also needs cloud clearances + 3 SM vis.
UA.III.B.K1k
k. Lightning Lightning hazard to people/equipment even miles from the cell — land and seek shelter.

UA.IV.A · Loading and Performance

IV.A

Task IV.A — Loading and Performance

UA.IV.A.K1
General loading and performance, including: Weight and balance affect stability, control authority, endurance, and wind tolerance. Stay within manufacturer limits.
UA.IV.A.K1a
a. Effects of loading changes Adding weight increases required power/AOA, reduces climb and endurance, raises stall speed for winged UA.
UA.IV.A.K1b
b. Balance, stability, and center of gravity CG too aft = less stable; CG too forward = nose-heavy / harder to flare or pitch up. Lateral balance also matters for multirotors.
UA.IV.A.K2
Importance and use of performance data to calculate the effect on the aircraft’s performance of an sUAS. Use performance data / charts (e.g., load factor vs bank). Higher bank → higher load factor → higher stall speed.

UA.V.A · Radio Communications

V.A · 1/2

Task V.A — Radio Communications

UA.V.A.K1
Airport operations with and without an operating control tower. Towered: ATC controls runway/taxi. Nontowered: pilots self-announce on CTAF. Know which you’re near.
UA.V.A.K2
The description and use of a Common Traffic Advisory Frequency (CTAF) to monitor manned aircraft communications. CTAF = frequency for advisory traffic calls at nontowered airports. Monitor to hear positions/intentions.
UA.V.A.K3
Recommended traffic advisory procedures used by manned aircraft pilots such as self-announcing of position and intentions. Typical calls: departing, downwind, base, final, clear of runway. Helps you predict manned traffic paths.
UA.V.A.K4
Aeronautical advisory communication station (UNICOM) and associated communication procedures used by manned aircraft pilots. UNICOM: airport advisory / services. Often same frequency as CTAF at small fields.

UA.V.A · Radio Communications

V.A · 2/2

Task V.A — Radio Communications

UA.V.A.K5
Automatic Terminal Information Service (ATIS). ATIS: recorded weather + airport info on a published frequency; updates hourly or as needed.
UA.V.A.K6
Aircraft call signs and registration numbers. Aircraft called by type + N-number (registration). Helps you track who is where in the pattern.
UA.V.A.K7
The phonetic alphabet. Phonetic alphabet: Alpha, Bravo, Charlie… used in callsigns and clearances.
UA.V.A.K8
Phraseology: altitudes, directions, speed, and time. Phraseology for altitudes (“four thousand”), headings, speeds, time (UTC/Zulu).

UA.V.B · Airport Operations

V.B · 1/3

Task V.B — Airport Operations

UA.V.B.K1
Types of airports such as towered, uncontrolled towered, heliport, and seaplane bases. Towered, nontowered, heliport, seaplane base — each has different traffic behavior.
UA.V.B.K2
ATC towers, such as ensuring the remote pilot can monitor and interpret ATC communications to improve situational awareness. Listening to tower/CTAF improves SA even when you are not talking.

UA.V.B.K4
Traffic patterns used by manned aircraft pilots. Standard left traffic unless right traffic indicated. Legs: upwind, crosswind, downwind, base, final.

Getting the license

Certificate path

  1. Study ACS topics (this deck).
  2. Create an FAA account / obtain tracking number as required for testing.
  3. Pass UAG knowledge test (≥70%) at a PSI center.
  4. Apply via IACRA → TSA security vetting → Temporary then plastic certificate.
  5. Complete recurrent training within every 24 calendar-months.
Eligibility: at least 16, English proficient, pass test (or eligible Part 61 path), pass security vetting.
